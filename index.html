Introduction to Water — H₂O

Overview

Water (chemical formula H2O) is one of the most important molecules for life. It is a simple molecule made of two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom. Despite its simplicity, water has unusual physical and chemical properties—many of which are crucial for biology, climate, and chemistry.

Key properties

  • Polarity: The oxygen end is slightly negative and the hydrogen ends are slightly positive — this creates hydrogen bonding.
  • High specific heat: Water resists temperature change and stabilizes environments.
  • Density anomaly: Ice is less dense than liquid water, so ice floats.
  • Universal solvent: Water dissolves many ionic and polar substances.

Everyday uses

  • Drinking, cooking, agriculture.
  • Industrial solvent and coolant.
  • Medium for biochemical reactions inside cells.
